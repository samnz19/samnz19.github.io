The ideal holiday destination

Byron Bay is very popular and well known holiday hotspot, located in northern NSW, with a population of around 30,000. It attracts over 1.7 million visitors per year, from young partying backpackers, to surfers, to adventure daredevils to mature nature enthusiasts. Who ever you are Byron is a great place to visit work or live! I really enjoyed it during my 5 years living and working there.

History of Byron

Captain Cook originally named Cape Byron after Admiral Byron. The first jetty was built in 1886, and the railway was connected in 1894, and Cavvanbah township became Byron Bay in 1894. Dairy farmers cleared more land and settled the area. From the 1930s a sand mining company extracted minerals from the beaches and from the 1950s Byron Bay Whaling Co. started capturing and killing whales until it closed in 1962.
